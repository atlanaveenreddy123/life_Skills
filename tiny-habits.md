### 2. How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P.

B = MAP means a behavior happens when Motivation, Ability, and Prompt come together.

- M (Motivation): how much you want to do the habit
- A (Ability): how easy the habit is to do
- P (Prompt): a reminder that tells you to do it
- We can make new habits easier by making them very small and simple so they are easy to do.

### 3. Why it is important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit? (This is the most important concept in today's topic. Whatever you celebrate becomes a habit)

- Celebrating after doing a small habit makes your brain feel happy. This happy feeling helps your brain remember the habit and makes you want to do it again. In simple words, what you celebrate becomes easier to repeat and turns into a strong habit.

### 5. What is the book's perspective about Identity?

- The book says that habits should focus on the kind of person you want to become, not just goals. For example, think “I am a healthy person” instead of only “I want to lose weight.” Small habits should match your identity and help shape who you become over time.

### 6. Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?

- The book explains that habits become easier when you:
- Reduce the steps
- Make the habit very simple and easy to start
- Remove anything that makes it hard or slow to do

### 7. Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?

To make a habit harder to do, you:

- Add more steps
- Make it less easy or convenient
- Increase effort or obstacles
