
## 1. What are the steps/stratagies to do Active Listening?
- Focus your attention on the speaker and the topic.
- Keep your personal opinions aside while listening.
- Pay attention to the speaker's body language and facial expressions.
- Use door openers.
- Paraphrase content accurately.
- Ask non-threatening questions.

## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?
  According to Fisher’s model, the key points of Reflective Listening are:

- Try to understand the speaker clearly.
- Repeat or restate what the speaker said in your own words to check if you understood correctly.
- Notice the speaker’s feelings, tone, and body language.
- Show that you are listening by giving small responses like “yes”, “okay”, or nodding.
- Ask questions if you need more clarity.
- Make sure both sides agree on the same meaning.

## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?
- Talk when others are talking
- Often have to ask people to repeat themselves
- Often interrupt others when they're talking
- Let your feelings for the speaker interfere with your listening ability
- Jump to conclusions before the speaker is finished talking
- Not Pay attention to the speaker's body language and facial expressions.
  
## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?
- Focus your attention on the speaker and the topic.
- Set aside your own feelings and prejudices.
- Pay attention to the speaker's body language and facial expressions.
- Use encouraging phrases to keep the conversation going.
- Ask simple and safe questions.
- Give short replies that show you understand.

## 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?
- When I don’t want to hurt someone’s feelings.
- When the situation feels uncomfortable and I avoid saying my opinion.
- When I think the other person will not listen or will get upset.
- When I want to keep peace in the conversation instead of arguing.

## 6. When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?
- When I feel stressed or irritated.
- When I think someone is not respecting my work or time.
- When people interrupt me or don’t let me speak.
- When I feel the situation is unfair and I react strongly.

## 7. When do you switch into Passive Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?
- When I don’t want to argue directly but I am upset.
- When someone ignores my boundaries or takes advantage.
- When I can’t express myself clearly, so I stay silent or sarcastic.
- When I expect people to understand my feelings without saying them.

## 8. How can you make your communication assertive?
- Speak clearly and directly about what I want or need.
- Use a calm tone and positive words instead of blaming.
- Listen to the other person and respect their point of view.
- Set boundaries politely and stay confident without being rude.
